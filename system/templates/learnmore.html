The Gift of Life

Every blood donation can save up to three lives. Learn how your simple act of kindness makes a world of difference.

Why Donate Blood?

Blood donation is one of the most significant contributions a person can make to society. It's a simple, safe way to make a big difference.

Save Lives

Your single donation can help multiple patients - from accident victims to cancer patients and new mothers.

1 donation = 3 lives saved

Health Benefits

Regular donation helps reduce harmful iron stores, stimulates new blood cell production, and provides a free health checkup.

Reduces cardiovascular risk

Community Impact

Blood donations strengthen community resilience, ensuring hospitals have adequate supply for emergencies and routine treatments.

Builds a healthier society

The Donation Process

Donating blood is quick, easy, and safe. Here's what you can expect when you become a donor.

1

Registration

You'll complete a donor registration form with basic information like your name, address, and phone number.

2

Health Screening

A staff member will check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure and hemoglobin level to ensure you can safely donate.

3

Confidential Interview

You'll answer some questions about your health history and places you've traveled to ensure donor safety.

4

Donation

The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes. You'll donate about a pint of blood.

5

Refreshment & Recovery

After donating, you'll enjoy snacks and drinks while you rest for 10-15 minutes before resuming your day.

Blood Donation Facts

Knowledge is power. Learn these important facts about blood donation.

Who Can Donate?

  • Age 17+ (16 with parental consent)
  • Weight at least 110 lbs (50 kg)
  • In good general health
  • No new tattoos/piercings in last 3 months

Donation Frequency

  • Whole blood: Every 56 days
  • Platelets: Every 7 days (max 24/year)
  • Plasma: Every 28 days
  • Double red cells: Every 112 days

Blood Components

  • Red cells: Carry oxygen (42 days shelf life)
  • Plasma: Clotting factors (1 year frozen)
  • Platelets: Stop bleeding (5 days shelf life)
  • Cryoprecipitate: Special clotting factors
