Blood Donation Eligibility Criteria

Blood donation is a safe and simple way to help save lives. However, there are some eligibility criteria that must be met in order to donate blood.

General Eligibility Criteria

  • You must be at least 17 years old (or 16 years old with parental consent, depending on state law).
  • You must weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kilograms).
  • You must be in good health and feeling well on the day of donation.
  • You must have a valid ID.

Specific Eligibility Criteria

  • You cannot donate blood if you have a cold, flu, or other infection.
  • You cannot donate blood if you have recently been tattooed or pierced.
  • You cannot donate blood if you have recently received a blood transfusion.
  • You cannot donate blood if you have recently traveled to a country with a high risk of malaria or other infectious diseases.

How to Donate Blood

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you can donate blood at a blood donation center. The donation process is typically very quick and easy.

First, you will be asked to complete a health history questionnaire. Then, you will have your blood pressure, pulse, and temperature checked. You will also have a small blood sample drawn to test for anemia.

If you pass all of the screening tests, you will be able to donate blood. The donation process itself takes about 15 minutes.

After you donate blood, you will be asked to rest for a few minutes. You will also be given a snack and a drink.

The Importance of Blood Donation

Blood donation is an important way to help save lives. Blood is needed for a variety of medical procedures, including surgery, childbirth, and treatment of trauma.

If you are able to donate blood, please consider doing so. Your donation could help save a life.
